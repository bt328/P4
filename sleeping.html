Sleeping

Sleep is essential for both physical and mental well-being, yet it is often overlooked in daily routines. Getting consistent, high-quality sleep helps regulate mood, improve memory, and support overall brain function. During sleep, the body repairs itself, strengthens the immune system, and processes information from the day. Without enough rest, individuals may experience increased stress, difficulty concentrating, and reduced emotional stability.

Healthy sleep habits, often referred to as good “sleep hygiene,” can significantly improve sleep quality. This includes maintaining a consistent sleep schedule, limiting screen time before bed, and creating a calm, comfortable sleep environment. Reducing caffeine intake in the evening and practicing relaxation techniques such as deep breathing or meditation can also help prepare the body for rest. Over time, these small changes can lead to more restorative sleep and better overall health.

Below is a chart containing the recommended amount of sleep based on age:

Age Range Recommended Daily Sleep
Infant 4-12 months 12-16 hours (including naps)
Toddler 1-2 years 11-14 hours (including naps)
Preschool 3-5 years 10-13 hours (including naps)
School-age 6-12 years 9-12 hours
Teens 13-18 years 8-10 hours
Adult 18 years and older 7 or more hours
